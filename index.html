About

PhD candidate in Computer Science. Research focuses on weakly supervised semantic segmentation with image-level labels, particularly interested in CAM refinement, noise robustness, and foundation model adaptation.

  • 2018–22B.S. · Computer Science[University]

Research

01 · Weakly Supervised Semantic Segmentation

Leveraging image-level labels to achieve pixel-level segmentation. Focus on CAM refinement, pseudo-label noise analysis, and self-supervised learning paradigms.

02 · Noise Robustness in WSSS

Understanding and mitigating the impact of pseudo-label noise on segmentation performance. Exploring uncertainty estimation, adaptive filtering, and consistency regularization.

03 · CLIP & Foundation Models for Segmentation

Adapting vision-language pre-trained models for dense prediction tasks. Investigating patch-text alignment and knowledge transfer without full fine-tuning.
